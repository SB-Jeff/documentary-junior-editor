### Project Summary

A B2B customer testimonial for Epicor (Eclipse ERP platform). Four speakers from a family-owned electrical distribution company with 12 locations across Northeast Ohio. The video covers business growth (quadrupled in 10-15 years), the role of Eclipse across every facet of operations, and forward-looking excitement about cloud migration and AI. Notable for its multi-generational business narrative and technology-forward positioning.

### Act Structure

**Act 1 — Legacy & Foundation:** Family business story — father ran it 40+ years, believed in technology. Chose Eclipse on peer recommendations. Quadrupled the business. Eclipse touches every part of daily operations.

**Act 2 — Innovation & Cloud:** The turning point — attending Epicor Insights conference, seeing AI capabilities, the immediate decision to move to the cloud. Security, data reliability, scalability. New API structure enabling digital competition and e-commerce integration.

**Act 3 — Solutions & Future:** Automation Studios enabling creative internal solutions. Eclipse as a tool for scalability — product, people, space decisions driven by data analytics. Technology as a strategic pillar. Closes with partnership commitment.

### What Worked Well

**Four speakers with distinct roles.** Michael (president, vision/legacy), Beth (operations, digital/e-commerce), Andrew (day-to-day problem solving), Jon (IT/infrastructure, data analytics). Each speaker owns a clear lane — no redundancy.

**The "aha moment" at the conference.** The Insights conference scene is a strong narrative device — two people hearing the same presentation and turning to each other with the same realization. It humanizes a technology decision.

**Forward-looking energy.** Unlike testimonials that focus primarily on solved problems, this one builds momentum toward what's next — cloud, AI, automation. The excitement feels genuine, not scripted.

### Rules That Emerged

**1. Four speakers is a strong number for multi-perspective testimonials.** Enough to show organizational breadth without creating confusion. Each speaker needs a distinct role and perspective.

**2. Conference/event moments can serve as narrative turning points.** The Insights conference scene works because it shows the decision happening in real time rather than describing it retrospectively.

**3. Forward-looking content should close the video.** When the customer is genuinely excited about where the technology is going, that energy makes a stronger close than reflecting on past success.

### Reference Value

Strong reference for multi-speaker B2B testimonials with a technology-forward narrative. Shows how to balance legacy/history with innovation/future. The four-speaker structure with distinct roles is a useful model. Also demonstrates how to handle a family business story across generations without it becoming a biography.
