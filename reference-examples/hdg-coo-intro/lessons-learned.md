### Project Summary

A new staff introduction video for Health Dimensions Group (HDG), a senior living and healthcare organization. Introduces Erin GaN as the new COO to an internal audience of ~3,000 team members across multiple communities. Single speaker throughout. The video establishes credibility through her 30 years of industry experience, builds rapport through shared values and genuine enthusiasm, and closes with a forward-looking team message. ~1:30 final length.

### Act Structure

**Act 1 — Identity & Background:** Who Erin is and what she brings. Name, title, 30 years of experience across nursing homes, assisted living, home care, hospice, and accountable care organizations. Establishes breadth and credibility quickly.

**Act 2 — Purpose & Philosophy:** Why she does this work. The industry matters, she finds purpose in it, she likes the business challenge, and she learns from everyone she meets. This section reveals character and values — not just credentials.

**Act 3 — Vision & Team:** Why HDG specifically, and what's ahead. Values alignment (hospitality, stewardship, integrity, respect, humor), growth ambition, and a direct message to the team — she's coming to the communities, she wants their ideas, and 3,000 team members means 3,000 times the opportunity. Closes with "We are going to go places as a team."

### What Worked Well

**Internal audience framing.** Unlike the Dr. Pan intro (external, patient-facing), this video speaks directly to colleagues. The closing sequence — meeting everybody, getting into communities, hoping people share ideas — is a direct address to the team. This gives the Edit Agent a model for how staff intros shift tone when the audience is internal.

**Values as a bridge.** Act 3 opens by naming HDG's organizational values and showing they match her personal values. This is an effective structural move for internal audience videos — it says "I already belong here" without saying it directly.

**Tight, natural pacing.** At ~1:30, this video is even shorter than the Dr. Pan intro and maintains a conversational feel throughout. No quote feels over-trimmed or artificially compressed.

### Rules That Emerged

**1. Internal vs. external audience changes the closing.** External-facing intros (Dr. Pan) close with an emotional payoff designed to motivate action (book a consultation). Internal-facing intros (HDG COO) close with a direct team message designed to build rapport and invite participation. The Edit Agent should ask about intended audience early and calibrate the Act 3 selection accordingly.

**2. Values alignment is a strong structural move for internal intros.** Naming the organization's values and connecting them to personal values is an effective bridge between "who I am" and "where we're going together."

### Reference Value

This is the second new staff introduction in the knowledge base and the first with an internal audience. Future projects of this type should reference it for:

- **Internal audience model:** How the act structure and especially Act 3 shift when speaking to colleagues rather than clients/patients
- **Values bridge technique:** Using organizational values as a structural transition into vision
- **Pacing benchmark:** A ~1:30 internal intro that feels natural and unhurried — useful calibration alongside the Dr. Pan ~1:45 external intro
- **Comparison with Dr. Pan:** Together, these two examples show the range of the New Staff Introduction type — external/patient-facing vs. internal/team-facing, physician vs. executive, clinical credibility vs. operational credibility
