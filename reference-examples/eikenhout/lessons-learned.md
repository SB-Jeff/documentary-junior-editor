### Project Summary

A B2B customer testimonial for Epicor featuring a 130-year-old distribution company. Three speakers cover multiple Epicor products — AP Automation, Warehouse Management System (WMS), and hosted BizTrac. The video balances product-specific stories with an overall partnership narrative. Eikenhout's scale and longevity give the testimonial particular credibility.

### Act Structure

**Act 1 — Legacy & Challenge:** A 130-year-old company staying relevant through technology. The old AP process was manual — paper invoices in bags, physical matching, manual entry, scanning. Labor-intensive and unscalable.

**Act 2 — Solution & Impact:** Three product stories woven together. AP Automation cut invoice processing time and allowed branch expansion without adding staff. WMS improved inventory accuracy (shrink dropped 50%), made training faster (onboard in days). Hosted BizTrac eliminated on-site server maintenance and midnight upgrades.

**Act 3 — Partnership & Forward Look:** Epicor's responsiveness and follow-through. "Acknowledge the problem, find a plan, deliver" — mirroring their own customer service philosophy. 21-year relationship. Closes with "always looking for what's next on the horizon."

### What Worked Well

**Multiple products, one narrative.** The video covers three distinct Epicor products without feeling like a product demo. Each product is introduced through a pain point and landed through a specific result. The products serve the story, not the other way around.

**The "before and after" specificity.** Concrete details make the transformation credible: invoices in bags, paper piles, midnight server upgrades vs. automated workflows, 50% shrink reduction, days to onboard.

**Three complementary speaker perspectives.** Each speaker covers different products and roles, giving the video breadth without redundancy. The intercut between speakers creates pacing variety.

### Rules That Emerged

**1. Multi-product testimonials should anchor each product to a specific pain point.** Without the pain-point anchor, product mentions blur together. Each product in this video has a clear "before" that makes the "after" land.

**2. Long-tenured customers should reference the length of the relationship.** "21 years" carries weight. When the customer has been with the vendor for a long time, that fact should be featured, not buried.

### Reference Value

Strong reference for multi-speaker, multi-product B2B testimonials. Shows how to weave multiple product stories into a single coherent narrative. The three-speaker structure and product-specific before/after pattern are transferable to similar projects.
