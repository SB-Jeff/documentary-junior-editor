### Project Summary

A B2B customer testimonial for Epicor (Profit 21 ERP + Elite Extra logistics). Two speakers from a janitorial supply distributor in the New York tri-state area. The video has a clear before/after structure centered on a single pain point — manual delivery routing and dispatch — and its transformation through Elite Extra. Notable for its vivid, relatable storytelling and specific metrics.

### Act Structure

**Act 1 — The Business & Pain Point:** Who Uneeda is — janitorial supply, five trucks, technology-driven despite the "you sell toilet paper" perception. Profit 21 as the core ERP. Then the pain point: manual delivery routing with printed paper stacks, vulnerable to a "strong breeze." The same topic came up over and over — how to route trucks better, how to reduce paper.

**Act 2 — Discovery & Implementation:** The Epicor Insights conference as the discovery moment — a presentation on Elite Extra. Immediate recognition that it would solve their logistics problems. Fast, flexible implementation. Responsive support.

**Act 3 — Impact & Future:** Everything on a computer screen now — map with red dots, one sheet of paper per driver, live tracking. Dispatching time cut 70-80%. Automatic delivery notifications to customers. Confidence that the system scales — "if our business doubled tomorrow."

### What Worked Well

**The "strong breeze" story.** The image of paper stacks getting scattered is immediately relatable and memorable. It makes the pain point visceral rather than abstract. Specific, vivid details are more powerful than general complaints.

**Hard metrics paired with human impact.** "70-80% reduction in dispatching time" is the metric. "Customer service has always been a huge thing for us — Elite Extra has only improved that" is the human impact. Both together are stronger than either alone.

**The conference discovery pattern.** Same pattern as Mars Electric — the Insights conference as the moment of discovery. This works because it shows initiative (they went looking for a solution) and genuine reaction (they couldn't believe what they were seeing).

### Rules That Emerged

**1. A single vivid pain-point story beats multiple abstract ones.** Uneeda's paper-stack-in-the-wind story is more memorable than listing five problems. When the pain point is concrete and visual, the solution lands harder.

**2. "Before and after" works best when the before is specific.** Not "routing was hard" but "we would print out all the orders, organize them into piles, and a strong breeze would mess up the entire process." The specificity makes the transformation believable.

**3. Scale confidence is a strong closer.** "If our business doubled tomorrow, I'm confident Elite Extra would handle the rest" — this forward-looking confidence reframes the product from a solution to a growth enabler.

### Reference Value

Excellent reference for two-speaker B2B testimonials with a clear before/after transformation story. The single-pain-point focus and vivid storytelling make this a strong model for projects where the customer has one dramatic improvement to highlight.
